Beach guide

All four main beaches form a walkable chain via a coastal pine forest path.

Sveti Stefan Beach
Pink-tinged pebble. Left side free (sunbeds ~€30/pair). Right side now publicly accessible (2026 settlement). Crystal clear turquoise. Go before 10am.
Miloçer Beach (King’s Beach)
Fine pink pebbles, “almost velvety.” 380m. Now public (2026). 10-min walk through Miloçer Forest Park. Bring supplies — limited facilities.
Queen’s Beach
Sand and pebble, 120m, sheltered cove. Aman guests only under 2026 settlement.
Przno Beach
Sandy-pebble crescent. Most crowded — arrive early. Restaurants on the beach. Atmospheric evenings when fishing boats return.
Petrovac Beach 20 min south
Sandy. Best sand beach in the region. More manageable than Budva.

Day trips

Kotor
35km, 45–50 min. UNESCO old town. Leave 7:30am, arrive 8am, leave by 11am before cruise ships. Fortress walls free before 8am, €8 after. Or return at dusk when the crowds leave.
Perast
45km, 55–60 min. Baroque village. Boat to Our Lady of the Rocks (€5, 5 min). Best late afternoon. Golden-hour light is exceptional.
Lovćen
60km, ~1.5h. Kotor Serpentine: 16 hairpin bends. Njegoš Mausoleum 1,657m (461 steps, €8). Njeguši village for pršut and cheese direct from producers.
Lake Skadar
50km, ~1h. Private boat tour ~€90/head for 5. Breakfast, lunch, swimming, bird watching, island monasteries. Lily pads, pelicans, clear swimming water.
Petrovac & Budva
Half-and-half day. Petrovac (sandy beach morning, Konoba Mediterraneo for lunch). Budva Old Town (sunset walls, dinner at Jadran).

Practical tips

Car & driving

  • Pick up at Tivat airport (~30km)
  • Consider two small cars for 5 of us
  • Book 3+ months ahead for August
  • Full insurance ~€10/day — worth it
  • Drive on the right, headlights always on
  • Blood alcohol limit: effectively zero

Money & mobile

  • Montenegro uses the Euro
  • Cards accepted in tourist areas; carry cash for markets
  • Tipping: 10% cash to server
  • Not in the EU — EU roaming doesn’t apply
  • Buy a local SIM at Tivat (€15–25 for 10–20GB) or use an eSIM
  • Supermarkets: Voli and Idea near Budva. HDL Laković for a big shop

Sample week

A rough shape for the week. Enough structure to hit the highlights, easy to swap a day trip for a pool day.

Day 1
Arrive. Fly to Tivat, drive to villa (~40 min). Afternoon swim at Sveti Stefan Beach. Dinner at Olive — right at the beach, no driving.
Day 2
Przno village. Walk the coastal forest path (20 min). Przno Beach. Lunch at Konoba Langust. Miloçer Forest Park. Pool evening.
Day 3
Kotor + Perast. Leave 7:30am. Fortress walls. Leave by 11am. Drive to Perast, Our Lady of the Rocks. Lunch in Perast. Pool afternoon.
Day 4
Lovćen & mountains. Kotor Serpentine. Pršut in Njeguši. Mausoleum (461 steps). Villa dinner with local wine.
Day 5
Full beach day. Miloçer Beach morning. Long lunch at Olive. Pool afternoon. Signature dinner at Çatovića Mlini (book weeks ahead).
Day 6
Lake Skadar. Private boat tour (~€90/head). Lily pads, pelicans, monasteries, swimming. Evening BBQ at the villa.
Day 7
Petrovac & Budva. Sandy beach morning at Petrovac. Konoba Mediterraneo for lunch. Final swim at Sveti Stefan. Evening: Budva Old Town at sunset, dinner at Jadran.
Day 8
Depart. Morning beach or pool. Tivat airport 30–40 min.
