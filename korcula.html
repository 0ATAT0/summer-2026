Wine guide

Korçula produces indigenous grapes we won’t find anywhere else. This is the real draw of the island — a genuine wine culture that rewards exploration.

The grapes

White

  • Grk — grows only in Lumbarda, sells out by October. The rarest grape on the island.
  • Pošip — full-bodied white from Cara & Smokvica. Croatia’s most celebrated white variety.
  • Rukatac — ancient white, grown on the island for over 1,000 years.

Red

  • Plavac Mali — robust red, genetically related to Zinfandel. Best from Pelješac’s Dingaç vineyards.

Wineries on Korçula

Small family operations. Tastings typically €12–20 per person. No need to book for most — just turn up.

Island wineries

  • Bire — Lumbarda, Grk specialist
  • Vitis — Lumbarda
  • Sabulum — Lumbarda
  • Popić — Lumbarda

Guided tour

  • “Slurp of Grk” — guided wine tour via Korçula Explorer. Covers multiple producers with transport.

Pelješac peninsula (15-min ferry)

Croatia’s premier red wine region is a short ferry hop away. Four world-class wineries, all doable in a day.

Grgić Vina
Founded by Mike Grgić, the Napa Valley legend who changed American wine at the 1976 Judgement of Paris. Walk-ins welcome. Plavac Mali and Pošip.
Matusko
Dingaç cellars — the most celebrated appellation in Croatia. Deep, powerful reds aged in oak.
Edivo Vina
The underwater winery. Wines aged for 14 months below the sea in amphorae on the seabed. Genuinely unique. Book ahead for the dive-and-taste experience.
Korta Katarina
Right at the Orebic ferry terminal. Six different tasting experiences. Convenient first or last stop on a Pelješac wine day.

Restaurant guide

Korçula’s food scene runs from a Michelin-starred destination to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Book the top spots well ahead for August.

Michelin star
LD Restaurant — Korçula Town
Six-course tasting menu with matching wines. Five consecutive years as Croatia’s most Wine Friendly restaurant. Book well ahead — this fills fast in season.
Tasting menu ~€120+, mains €40–70
Bib Gourmand + Green Star
Konoba Mate — Pupnat (15 min from town)
Family konoba under fig trees since 1974. Home-cured charcuterie, aged goat’s cheese, handmade ravioli with sage. Walk-ins impossible in season. Book: +385 20 717 109
Konoba Belin — Zrnovo (4km from town)
Hand-rolled Žrnovski makaruni — the island’s signature pasta, unique to this village. Cooking classes available if we want to learn the technique.
~€45–50/person
Konoba Maha — Zrnovo
Two brothers, since 2003. Modern Dalmatian cooking, handmade cocktails with domestic plants. Peka (lamb or octopus slow-cooked under a bell lid — must order 24 hours ahead).
Restaurant Filippi — Korçula Town promenade
Michelin-recognised. Modern Dalmatian cuisine with sea views across the Pelješac channel. Good mid-tier option for a town evening.
Vrnik Arts Club — Vrnik islet (by boat)
Tiny island, tranquil terrace in an old stone quarry. Swordfish ceviche, local cheese with pršut, fresh seafood. Scenic and uncrowded — worth the boat trip.

Pelješac dining

Saints Hills — Zagruda, Pelješac
Fine dining at a vineyard. Wine pairing menus with views over the peninsula. A strong lunch stop on a Pelješac wine day.
Korta Katarina — Orebic (at the ferry port)
Six tasting experiences at the winery. Pair with lunch. Convenient location right where the ferry docks.

Beach guide

Korçula’s beaches are pebble and cove rather than long sand strips. The trade-off is crystal-clear water with excellent visibility. Arrive early in August — parking fills fast at the popular spots.

Vela Pržina, Lumbarda
Sandy — the largest on the island. Shallow, excellent for everyone. Very busy in August; arrive before 9am. 15-minute drive from Korçula Town.
Bilin Žal, Lumbarda
Sand and small pebble mix. Longer stretch than Pržina, moderate crowds. Beach bars for drinks and snacks.
Pupnatska Luka
Smooth pale pebble horseshoe bay. Most photographed beach on the island. 25–30 minute drive from town. Two seasonal konobas for lunch. Parking fills before 10am in August.
Baçva
Larger pebbles, south coast. Low crowds. Excellent snorkelling with 15m+ visibility. Steep entry into the water — not ideal for Mum and Dad.
Proizd Island
Uninhabited island, 20-minute taxi boat from Vela Luka. Clearest water in the region. One restaurant. Total peace.
Banje
Pebble, 5-minute walk from the Old Town. Convenient for a morning dip before breakfast or a late-afternoon swim.
Cruise ship note: August sees roughly 5 cruise calls to Korçula. When a ship docks, the Old Town is overwhelmed for 4–6 hours. Plan to be at a beach or wine village on those days — check the port schedule when we arrive.

Day trips

Four strong options that each offer something different. Mljet is the standout — the best nature day of the week.

Mljet
Catamaran 35–45 min. National park with salt lakes. Bike around them, take the lake boat to the Benedictine monastery. ~€20–25 return + €15–25 park entry. Full day. Best nature day of the week.
Pelješac
15-min car ferry. Wine day: Korta Katarina, Grgić Vina (walk-ins, ~30 min), Matusko (Dingaç cellars), Edivo (underwater winery — book ahead). Saints Hills vineyard lunch. Works for all ages.
Hvar
Catamaran 1–1.5h. Venetian architecture, hilltop fortress, lavender fields. More crowded and party-oriented — better for the younger contingent. Book ferry ahead — sells out in August.
Proizd
50-min drive to Vela Luka + 20-min taxi boat. Uninhabited island, clearest water in the area. One restaurant. Total peace and the best swimming of the trip.

Getting there

Route: Fly London → Dubrovnik (easyJet/BA, ~20 flights per week, 2h 25–55m). Private minibus to Orebic ferry (€80–120). 15-min car ferry to Korçula. Total: ~4.5–5 hours door to door. August average temperature: 34°C.

Car hire

  • Pick up on the island rather than the mainland
  • Avax — Vela Luka location
  • Korkyra — Korçula Town
  • Book 3+ months ahead for August

On arrival

  • Ferry runs Orebic – Domincče (3km from Korçula Town)
  • Catamaran also runs Dubrovnik – Korçula direct
  • Car essential for beaches and wine villages
  • Island is ~47km long — compact enough for day trips

Sample week

A week that balances wine, beaches, food, and day trips — with enough flexibility to adjust based on weather and cruise ship schedules.

Day 1
Arrive. Fly to Dubrovnik, private transfer (~4.5h). Settle in, swim at the villa, grocery run. Villa dinner — pick up local wine and cheese on the way.
Day 2
Korçula Town. Banje beach in the morning. Walk the walls, cathedral, Marco Polo house. Lunch at Filippi. Bilin Žal for an afternoon swim. Evening: LD Restaurant — the Michelin highlight of the trip.
Day 3
Wine villages. Late breakfast at Konoba Belin in Zrnovo. Drive to Smokvica & Cara for Pošip tastings. Afternoon at Pupnatska Luka beach. Fresh fish at the beach konoba.
Day 4
Mljet. Catamaran at 8:30am. Bike the salt lakes. Monastery island. Swim in Veliko Jezero. Return ~5pm. Best nature day.
Day 5
Pelješac wine day. Ferry to Orebic. Korta Katarina first, then Grgić Vina. Saints Hills vineyard lunch. Optional: Edivo underwater winery in the afternoon.
Day 6
Lumbarda & Mate. Sandy beach at Vela Pržina (arrive 9am). Grk tasting at Bire or Vitis. Evening: Konoba Mate — the Michelin Bib Gourmand. Don’t miss the ravioli.
Day 7
Hvar or Proizd. Catamaran to Hvar for the younger group, or taxi boat to Proizd for crystal-clear swimming. Farewell sunset drinks in the Old Town.
Day 8
Depart. Allow 5+ hours to Dubrovnik Airport. Buy olive oil & Pošip before leaving — we won’t find these at home.
