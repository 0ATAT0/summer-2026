The fishing village of Przno, ten minutes north of Budva but a world apart, ticks every box. Fishermen still work from the beach in the morning. Four distinct beaches are within walking distance. The restaurant scene — anchored by Drago (est. 1967) — is genuinely excellent. Zero cruise ships. And your £7–10k budget buys you the top tier of Montenegrin villa stock, because Montenegro is 23% cheaper than Croatia.

Glitz check: The Aman resort on Sveti Stefan island is ultra-luxury but discreetly so — invisible if you’re not a guest. The public beach flanking the causeway was open and free as of 2024. The surrounding area is authentic fishing village, not oligarch territory.

Beach Guide

All four main beaches form a walkable chain connected by a coastal pine forest path — one of the genuine pleasures of the area.

Sveti Stefan Beach — Pink-tinged pebble (calcium shell fragments). Left/public side is free (sunbeds ~€30/pair). Right side now publicly accessible following the 2026 Aman settlement. Moderate-high crowds in August. Go before 10am for photography. Crystal clear turquoise water.
Miloçer Beach (King’s Beach) — Fine pink pebbles, “almost velvety.” 380m long. Now publicly accessible (2026 settlement). 10-min walk through Miloçer Forest Park (century-old royal pines). Limited facilities — bring supplies. The forest walk is itself a highlight.
Queen’s Beach — Sand and pebble, 120m, sheltered cove with cypress and olive trees. Aman guests only under the 2026 settlement. The most secluded of the four.
Przno Beach — Sandy-pebble mix, crescent bay. Partially reserved by Maestral Resort; public portion free. Most crowded of the four — arrive early. Seafront restaurants directly on beach (Langust, Blanche). Atmospheric in the evening with fishing boats returning.
Petrovac Beach (20 min south) — Sandy — the best sand beach in the region. Busier but more manageable than Budva. Beach bars, two small pine-forested islands visible offshore. Combine with lunch at Konoba Mediterraneo.

Day Trips

Kotor
35km, 45–50 min. UNESCO walled medieval city. Genuinely one of the most beautiful old towns in Europe. Crucial: leave by 7:30am, arrive 8am, leave by 11am before cruise ships overwhelm it. Hike the San Giovanni fortress walls (free before 8am, €8 after). Cathedral of St Tryphon. Or return at dusk when cruise crowds leave — magical.
Perast
45km, 55–60 min. Tiny Baroque village on the Bay of Kotor. Take the boat to Our Lady of the Rocks (€5 return, 5 min) — an artificial island built by sailors adding stones over centuries. Best in late afternoon/evening when cruise passengers are gone. Golden-hour light on the bay is exceptional.
Lovćen
60km, ~1.5h. The Kotor Serpentine: 16 hairpin bends, jaw-dropping views. Njegoš Mausoleum at 1,657m (461 steps, €8). Stop at Njeguši village to buy local pršut and cheese direct from producers. Optional add-on: Cetinje (Montenegro’s royal capital). Full day — allow 8–10 hours. Manageable in a standard rental car.
Skadar Lake
50km, ~1h. Largest lake in the Balkans. Private boat tour recommended (~€90/head for 5 people) — includes breakfast, lunch, swimming, bird watching, island monastery visits. Depart from Virpazar village. Lily pad fields, Dalmatian Pelicans, clear swimming water.
Petrovac + Budva
Half-and-half day. Petrovac (20 min south) for a sandy beach morning and lunch at Konoba Mediterraneo. Budva Old Town (15 min north) for an evening walk along the medieval walls at sunset, dinner at Jadran.

Practical Tips

Car Rental

  • Pick up at Tivat airport (~30km to Sveti Stefan)
  • Consider two small cars or one 7-seater for 5 people
  • Book 3+ months ahead for August
  • Get full comprehensive insurance (€10/day)
  • Drive on the right. Headlights always on. Blood alcohol effectively zero (0.03%)

Money

  • Montenegro uses the Euro
  • Cards widely accepted in tourist areas
  • Carry cash for beach vendors, smaller konobas, markets
  • Tipping: 10% for good service, in cash to server
  • “Kuver” (cover charge) €1–2.50/person is normal, not a scam

Mobile & Roaming

  • Montenegro is NOT in the EU — EU roaming rules don’t apply
  • O2 out-of-bundle data can be £18/100MB
  • Best: buy a local SIM at Tivat airport (€15–25 for 10–20GB)
  • Or load an eSIM before departure
  • Coastal 4G coverage is excellent

Supermarkets

  • Voli and Idea are the main chains near Budva
  • HDL Laković hypermarket for a big stock-up
  • Do a main shop in Budva on arrival day
  • Tap water is safe to drink

If the group specifically wants an island holiday with wine culture, Korçula is the answer. The island produces indigenous grapes you won’t find anywhere else — Grk (almost unique to Lumbarda), Pošip, and Plavac Mali. The food scene runs from Michelin-starred LD Restaurant to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Crowds are real in August but manageable if you’re villa-based outside the old town, visiting in the evening after cruise boats leave.

Croatia costs more. Your £7–10k budget is realistic but at the ceiling for a 5-bed pool villa in August. Croatia has seen ~50% price inflation over three years and is now the second most expensive Mediterranean destination after France.

Wine Guide

The Grapes

  • Grk (white) — grows only in Lumbarda. Female-only vine requiring cross-pollination. Light greenish-gold, complex, subtle bitter finish. Extremely limited — sells out by October
  • Pošip (white) — from Cara & Smokvica. Full-bodied, notes of apricot and almond. Croatia’s most celebrated white internationally
  • Plavac Mali (red) — dominant Dalmatian red. Rich, robust tannins, high alcohol. Genetically related to Zinfandel
  • Rukatac/Maraština (white) — ancient variety, 1,000+ years on the island. Light, balanced, pairs with shellfish

Wineries to Visit

  • Korçula: Bire, Vitis, Sabulum, Popic (all small family, Lumbarda/central). Tasting flights €12–20
  • “Slurp of Grk” tour — Korçula Explorer, guided Lumbarda producers tour with transport
  • Pelješac: Grgic Vina (Napa legend, walk-ins welcome), Matusko (Dingaç tasting), Edivo Vina (underwater winery — wines aged 14m below sea)
  • Korta Katarina (Orebic) — right at the ferry terminal, no driving needed

Beach Guide

Vela Pržina, Lumbarda — Sandy. Largest sandy beach on the island. Shallow 30–50m — ideal for all ages. Very busy August; arrive before 9am. 15-min drive from Old Town. Sunset views toward Mljet. Grk wineries walkable.
Bilin Žal, Lumbarda — Sand/small pebble. Longer and shallower than Vela Pržina. Moderate crowds. Good afternoon alternative. Beach bars, showers, toilets.
Pupnatska Luka — Smooth pale pebble in horseshoe bay. 25–30 min drive from town. Two seasonal konobas for grilled fish. Most photographed beach on the island. Pine and oak shade. Parking fills before 10am in August.
Baçva — Larger pebbles, south coast. Low crowds even in August. Excellent snorkelling (15m visibility). Steep entry — confident swimmers.
Proizd Island — Uninhabited island, 20-min taxi boat from Vela Luka. Smooth white rock coves. Clearest water in the region. One restaurant. Full-day trip recommended.
Banje — Pebble/concrete, 5-min walk from Old Town walls. Convenient morning dip. Moderate crowds. Great backdrop.

Cruise ship note: August typically brings ~5 cruise ship calls. When ships dock, Old Town is overwhelmed for 4–6 hours. Plan to be at a beach or wine village on those days.

Day Trips

Mljet
Catamaran 35–45 min from Korçula Town. National Park with salt lakes. Rent bikes, kayak, take the lake boat to the Benedictine monastery on Sveta Marija island. Exceptionally beautiful and quiet. ~€20–25 return + €15–25 park entry. Full day, depart ~8:30am. Best day of the week for natural beauty.
Pelješac
15-min car ferry from Domince. Wine day: Korta Katarina (at ferry port), Grgic Vina (walk-ins, 30 min drive), Matusko (Dingaç cellars), Edivo (underwater winery — book ahead). Lunch at Saints Hills vineyard. Works for all ages.
Hvar
Catamaran 1–1.5h. Venetian architecture, hilltop fortress, great market. Significantly more crowded and party-oriented than Korçula. Better for the younger contingent. Arrive early, leave mid-afternoon. Book ferry ahead — sells out in August.
Proizd
50-min drive to Vela Luka + 20-min taxi boat. Uninhabited island. Clearest water in the area. One restaurant. Total peace. Best if the group wants a secluded swimming day over sightseeing.

Getting There — Detailed

Recommended route: Fly London → Dubrovnik (easyJet/BA, ~20 weekly departures, 2h 25–55m). Private minibus transfer to Orebic/Domince ferry (€80–120 for 5). 15-min car ferry to Korçula. Total: ~4.5–5 hours door to villa.

Car hire: Easier to pick up on the island (Avax, Smart Rent, Prizmic) than taking a mainland hire car onto the ferry. Book March–April for August — island fleet is small.

The wildcard. Montenegro’s most southerly town, near the Albanian border, with a completely different character to the rest of the coast. Strong Ottoman/Albanian cultural influence, a cliff-top old town with genuine history, and — crucially — Velika Plaža: a 14km stretch of fine sand that is extraordinary and rare on the Adriatic. Zero cruise ships, genuinely local food at half the price of the Budva Riviera, and your £7–10k budget is borderline overkill here.

The caveats: 1.5 hours from Tivat airport. Infrastructure is rawer — not polished resort territory. Villa stock is thinner and less well-documented by UK agencies. Very hot in August (35°C+). But if the group wants maximum authenticity, this is it.

Cultural context: Ulcinj is an Albanian town inside Montenegro — over 70% of the population is ethnically Albanian. You’ll see Albanian signs alongside Montenegrin, hear the call to prayer from mosques, and eat Albanian dishes you won’t find elsewhere in Montenegro. The Albanian hospitality code (besa) means locals are unusually warm to visitors. The Old Town (Stari Grad) is on the UNESCO Tentative List.

Town centre & waterfront

Bazar — Best value in town. Pasta €4, pizza €5, fish €25/kg. Genuinely local crowd. Not glamorous but authentic. €.
Higo — Premium steaks including Wagyu/Kobe. Pinjiš pine forest above a cove. Highest-end option in town. For a celebratory dinner. €€€€.

Ada Bojana & Bojana River

Miško — Destination restaurant
Oldest fish restaurant on Ada Bojana. Legendary fish soup (riblja çorba), octopus carpaccio, black risotto. Wooden cabin with river views. Lobster tank. Worth the trip even from Budva.
Kod Ranka (Ranko’s Tavern) — Stilt house on the opposite bank of the Bojana River. Famous for škampi na buzaru (shrimps braised in wine and garlic). Access by boat. Extraordinary setting.

Valdanos area

Mashtra Restaurant — At the Olive House. Ancient olive grove terrace with sea views over Valdanos inlet. Fresh Adriatic fish, seasonal pasta, beautifully plated. Book ahead for August. The finest setting in the region.

Dishes to seek: bamije (veal & okra stew), riblja çorba (fish stew), škampi na buzaru, burek (filo pastry — best at Albanian bakeries for breakfast), octopus carpaccio, black risotto.

Beach Guide

Velika Plaža (Long Beach) — 12–13km of continuous fine grey sand. The longest stretch on the Adriatic. Extremely shallow for 50–100m — ideal for all. Northern section is family-friendly; southern tip is the kitesurfing zone with beach bars and a bohemian crowd. The scale means it never feels overcrowded (150,000 person capacity). Water clarity: 56m visibility. The sand reportedly has therapeutic mineral properties.
Best for: Everyone — parents relax on calm northern stretch, 30-year-olds kitesurf at the south end.
Mala Plaža (Town Beach) — 376m crescent of fine sand directly below the Old Town walls. Walking distance from restaurants. Packed in August. Shallow, family-friendly.
Best for: Quick morning dips around Old Town exploration.
Ada Bojana — 1,880m of soft grey sand on a river island at the Albanian border. Swim in the river (fresh) or the sea (salt). Wooden pile-dwelling houses, fish restaurants right on the river. Horseback riding at sunset on the sand dunes. Moderate crowds.
Best for: The couple and brother for the atmosphere; parents for the riverside restaurants.
Valdanos — 800m of gravelly pebbles in a sheltered cove. Surrounded by one of the largest ancient olive groves in Montenegro (100,000+ trees, some 2,000 years old). Low crowds, peaceful. Deeper water, great for snorkelling. Mashtra restaurant sits above in the olive groves.
Best for: Parents wanting shade and peace; combine with Mashtra lunch.
Crystal Beach — Pristine secluded cove. Marine Protected Area. Access by boat or overgrown footpath. No facilities.
Best for: The 27 and 30-year-olds looking for adventure.
Liman I & II — Small coves directly below the castle walls. Pebble/concrete with ladder access. Swimming under medieval walls. Low crowds. Atmospheric.

Day Trips

Stari Bar
30km, ~35 min. Ruins of a medieval walled city — destroyed in the 1878 war and 1979 earthquake. Open-air museum of remarkable scale. 11th-century fortress, Cathedral ruins, Ottoman hammam, the 2,000+ year old olive tree. Easy half-day, very low tourist footfall. Combine with Bar town for lunch.
Skadar Lake
45–60 min drive to Virpazar. Largest lake in the Balkans. 2–4 hour boat tours with pelicans, water lilies, crumbling island monasteries. Temperatures on the water are more bearable than shore. Virpazar has excellent local wine (Crmnica region, Montenegro’s best reds).
Shkodër
~30km south, 45–60 min with border. Closest Albanian city. Rozafa Castle (dramatic fortress, views into both countries). Old Bazaar for coffee and street food. Mesi Bridge (18th-century Ottoman). Check car rental allows Albania — some prohibit it; others charge €20–30 supplement. Border queues in August: 20–40 min. Most culturally distinctive day trip.
Sveti Stefan
1.5–2h north. Montenegro’s most photographed view. Combine with Budva Old Town (medieval walls, sunset walk). The contrast with Ulcinj will confirm you made the right choice.

The Heat Factor

August in Ulcinj: 29–32°C average, peaks of 35–38°C. UV index 8 (Very High). Ulcinj runs hotter than Kotor Bay due to its southern latitude.

The fix: Adopt the local siesta rhythm. Beach 8am–12pm, long lunch + villa + AC from 12pm–5pm, evening activities from 5pm onwards, dinner at 8–10pm (normal local hour). Valdanos is the only beach with genuine shade (olive groves). AC in all bedrooms is non-negotiable for mid-60s parents.

Practical Tips

Getting There

  • Tivat Airport to Ulcinj: ~120km, 1.5–2h drive south along the coast
  • Consider private transfer for arrival (~€80–120)
  • Road is paved E65/M2.4, two-lane, scenic but slow in places
  • August traffic Budva–Bar can add 15–30 min

Money & Language

  • Euro (€) — same as rest of Montenegro
  • Cards accepted at major restaurants; cash needed for bakeries, market stalls, boat trips
  • Albanian is the primary spoken language; English is sufficient for tourism
  • Useful: Faleminderit (thank you), Ju lutem (please)

Sample Week

Day 1
Arrival. Transfer from Tivat (~1h45). Pool through the heat. Evening: Mala Plaža for a sunset swim. Dinner at Bazar for an affordable, authentic first meal — get the fish by the kilo.
Day 2
Old Town immersion. Morning 8–11am: explore Stari Grad — ramparts, castle, Corsairs Museum. Albanian burek and coffee from a bakery. Mala Plaža swim. Afternoon siesta. Evening: Antigona for sunset on the cliffside terrace.
Day 3
Velika Plaža + kitesurfing. Drive to Long Beach by 8am. Parents on the northern family stretch. Couple/brother to the southern kitesurfing zone (Ka’banya school, ~€60–80 intro lesson). Midday retreat to beach club. Siesta. Evening: La Guitarra — order the bamije.
Day 4
Valdanos + Mashtra. Morning at Valdanos Beach (olive grove shade, snorkelling). Walk up to Mashtra restaurant for a long slow lunch in the ancient olive groves — the meal of the week. Pool afternoon. Light supper at the villa.
Day 5
Stari Bar + Lake Skadar. Leave 8am. 2 hours exploring the medieval ruins and the 2,000-year-old olive tree. Drive to Virpazar for a boat trip on the lake (2–3h). Lunch in Virpazar, try local Vranac red wine. Back mid-afternoon. Evening: Miško on Ada Bojana (book ahead).
Day 6
Shkodër, Albania. Leave 8am, 45–60 min to the border. Rozafa Castle, Old Bazaar for coffee and street food, Mesi Bridge. Back by 2pm. Pool afternoon. Evening: Dulcinea for the best formal dinner of the week.
Day 7
Ada Bojana slow day. Walk the island, swim in river-meets-sea. Optional: horseback riding at sunset on the sand dunes (~€25–40/hr). Farewell dinner: Kod Ranka — stilt house on the river, škampi na buzaru, sunset over the Albanian mountains.
Day 8
Departure. Allow 2+ hours to Tivat Airport.

The Bottom Line

Sveti Stefan / Przno is the answer. It’s the rare spot that satisfies everyone in a mixed-age group: beautiful beaches the parents can walk to, excellent local restaurants, authentic fishing-village character, no cruise ships, no oligarch glitz, and the budget stretches 20–25% further than Croatia. Korçula is the backup if the group wants a Croatian island with wine culture. Skip Kotor, Budva, Tivat, and Krk entirely in August.
