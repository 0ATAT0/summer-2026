Every suggestion, at a glance

Here’s what we found for each place on Mum’s list — and the Croatian islands too.

Montenegro
Perast
Day trip
Stunning Venetian village. Excellent evening restaurants. But no beaches and almost no villa stock — visit for dinner from a base elsewhere.
Kotor
Avoid
4–6 cruise ships daily in August. 15,000+ day-trippers into a town of 23,000. Local restaurant owners close up because “the season kills the magic.”
Luštica Bay
Wrong fit
Good news: it’s not the cruise ship dock (that’s Kotor). Bad news: a purpose-built luxury resort, still partly under construction. One reviewer called it “a soulless ghost town.”
Budva
Avoid
Montenegro’s party capital. 45% of all its tourism. Packed sun-loungers, loud music, Eastern European party crowd. The old town is described as “tacky.”
Sveti Stefan
Top pick
Przno fishing village. Four beaches, superb food, no cruise ships, completely authentic. Our budget buys the top tier here.
Ulcinj
Recommended
Most authentic spot on the entire list. Ottoman old town, 14km sandy beach, genuinely excellent cheap seafood. Rawer infrastructure, further from the airport.
Tivat
Fails brief
Porto Montenegro marina = documented oligarch yacht parking. Russian superyachts were reported here even during sanctions. The town behind it is fine, but the marina is unavoidable.
Kolašin
Day trip
Beautiful mountains, genuinely peaceful. But not coastal. Worth a day excursion — Biogradska Gora is one of Europe’s last virgin forests.
Croatian Islands
Korçula
Best island
Genuine food & wine culture (Michelin-starred LD, family konobas since 1974), indigenous grapes. Manageable crowds if we base outside the old town.
Braç
Backup
Best logistics (50-min ferry from Split). Deepest villa stock. But Zlatni Rat beach is a zoo in August. Would need to base near Milna, not Bol.
Lošinj
Awkward
Least crowded of the four islands. But: no commercial airport, nearly four hours from Rijeka, thinner villa stock, food scene drifts Japanese/Greek rather than local Croatian.
Krk
Eliminate
Bridge to the mainland means car-crowded mass tourism. “Insanely touristy” in August. Wrong crowd entirely.
01
Montenegro

Sveti Stefan / Przno

The fishing village of Przno, ten minutes north of Budva but a world apart. Fishermen still work from the beach in the morning. Four beaches are within walking distance. The restaurant scene is genuinely excellent. Zero cruise ships. And our budget buys the top tier here, because Montenegro is 23% cheaper than Croatia.

Getting there

  • Miloçer Beach — royal pines, pink pebbles
  • Przno Beach — village beach, restaurants right there

  • Drago (Przno) — est. 1967, local institution

02
Croatia

Korçula Island

The best option if we specifically want an island holiday with wine culture. Korçula produces indigenous grapes you won’t find anywhere else — Grk (almost unique to Lumbarda), Pošip, and Plavac Mali. The food scene runs from Michelin-starred LD to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Crowds are real in August but manageable from a rural base.

Value
Croatia costs more. Our £7–10k budget is realistic but at the ceiling for a 5-bed pool villa in August. Croatia has seen roughly 50% price inflation over three years and is now the second most expensive Mediterranean destination after France.

Getting there

  • Fly to Dubrovnik (easyJet/BA, ~20 weekly)
  • Private minibus to Orebic ferry (€80–120)
  • 15-min car ferry to Korçula
  • ~4.5–5h total from London

Budget

  • £7–10k gets a quality 5-bed pool villa
  • Not huge choice — book early
  • Dining: €80+ for two at upscale spots
  • Croatia is 23% pricier than Montenegro

Beaches

  • Pupnatska Luka — white pebble horseshoe bay
  • Vela Pržina (Lumbarda) — sandy, family-friendly
  • Baçva — secluded cliff cove, great snorkelling
  • Proizd island — clearest water in the region

Key restaurants

  • LD Restaurant — Michelin star, tasting menu
  • Konoba Mate (Pupnat) — Bib Gourmand + Green Star
  • Konoba Belin (Zrnovo) — hand-rolled makaruni
  • Filippi — Michelin-recognised, sea views

03
Montenegro

Ulcinj

The wildcard. Montenegro’s most southerly town, near the Albanian border, with a completely different character to the rest of the coast. Over 70% of the population is ethnically Albanian. Ottoman old town on the UNESCO Tentative List. And Velika Plaža: a 14km stretch of fine sand — extraordinary and rare anywhere on the Adriatic. Zero cruise ships, genuinely local food at half the price, and our budget is borderline overkill here.

The caveats: 1.5 hours from Tivat airport. Infrastructure is rawer — this is not polished resort territory. Villa stock is thinner. Very hot in August (35°C+). But if we want maximum authenticity, this is it.

Getting there

  • Fly to Tivat (easyJet/BA direct)
  • Drive south: ~1.5 hours (120km)
  • Coastal road, scenic but slow
  • Car is essential here

Budget

  • £7–10k is overkill — exceptional value
  • Fresh fish from €25/kg
  • Restaurant dinners very affordable
  • Could easily spend £5k and live like royalty

Beaches

  • Velika Plaža — 14km fine sand, extraordinary
  • Valdanos — sheltered pebble, olive groves, snorkelling
  • Ada Bojana — river island, wild and quiet
  • Mala Plaža — town beach below the old town walls

Key restaurants

  • Antigona — cliff-top sunset terrace, old town
  • Miško — legendary fish soup, Ada Bojana
  • La Guitarra — bamije (Albanian veal & okra stew)
  • Mashtra — olive grove terrace above Valdanos

Montenegro vs Croatia

Both work. Here’s how they compare for what we’re after.

Montenegro

Value
23% cheaper. Our budget goes much further.
Crowds
2.6M tourists/year total.
Flights
easyJet/BA direct to Tivat.
Food
Excellent seafood. Less wine culture.
Beaches
Ulcinj has rare sand. Rest is rocky/pebble.
Feel
Coastal mainland. No island option.

Croatia

Value
Pricier. £7–10k is ceiling, not floor.
Crowds
21M tourists/year. Heavy saturation.
Flights
Abundant to Split & Dubrovnik.
Food
Stronger wine scene. Korçula especially.
Beaches
Mostly pebble. Some sand on Braç.
Feel
True archipelago experience.

Practical notes

Book early

August peak means quality 5-bed villas book 4–6 months ahead. Don’t wait past February.

Car rental

Essential at all three picks. Book with the villa, not at the airport desk.

Where to book

montenegrovillas.com, oliverstravels.com, CV Villas, croatianvillaholidays.com

Flights

easyJet to Tivat (Montenegro) or Split/Dubrovnik (Croatia). All direct from London.

So what do we think?

Sveti Stefan / Przno is our recommendation. Beautiful beaches we can walk to, excellent local restaurants, authentic fishing-village character, no cruise ships, no glitz, and the budget stretches 20–25% further than Croatia. Korçula is the pick if we want a Croatian island with wine culture. Ulcinj is the adventure option. All three would be a brilliant week.
