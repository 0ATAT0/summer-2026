Beach guide

All four main beaches form a walkable chain connected by a coastal pine forest path.

Sveti Stefan Beach
Pink-tinged pebble (calcium shell fragments). Left side free (sunbeds ~€30/pair). Right side now publicly accessible (2026 settlement). Crystal clear turquoise water. Go before 10am.
Miloçer Beach (King’s Beach)
Fine pink pebbles, “almost velvety.” 380m long. Now publicly accessible. 10-min walk through Miloçer Forest Park (century-old royal pines). Bring supplies — limited facilities. The forest walk is itself a highlight.
Queen’s Beach
Sand and pebble, 120m, sheltered cove with cypress and olive trees. Aman guests only under the 2026 settlement. The most secluded of the four.
Przno Beach
Sandy-pebble mix, crescent bay. Most crowded of the four — arrive early. Restaurants directly on the beach. Atmospheric in the evening when fishing boats return.

Day trips

Kotor
35km, 45–50 min. UNESCO walled medieval city. Genuinely one of Europe’s most beautiful old towns. Leave by 7:30am, arrive 8am, leave by 11am before cruise ships overwhelm it. Hike the fortress walls (free before 8am, €8 after). Or return at dusk when crowds leave — magical.
Perast
45km, 55–60 min. Tiny Baroque village on the bay. Boat to Our Lady of the Rocks (€5 return, 5 min) — an artificial island built by sailors over centuries. Best in late afternoon when cruise passengers are gone. Golden-hour light is exceptional.
Lovćen
60km, ~1.5h. The Kotor Serpentine: 16 hairpin bends, jaw-dropping views. Njegoš Mausoleum at 1,657m (461 steps, €8). Stop at Njeguši village to buy pršut and cheese direct from producers. Full day — 8–10 hours.
Skadar
50km, ~1h. Largest lake in the Balkans. Private boat tour (~€90/head for 5) includes breakfast, lunch, swimming, bird watching, island monasteries. Lily pad fields, pelicans, clear swimming water.

Practical tips

Car & driving

  • Pick up at Tivat airport (~30km)
  • Consider two small cars for 5 people
  • Book 3+ months ahead for August
  • Get full insurance (€10/day)
  • Drive on the right, headlights always on

Money & mobile

  • Montenegro uses the Euro
  • Cards accepted in tourist areas; carry cash for markets
  • Tipping: 10% for good service, cash to server
  • Not in the EU — EU roaming doesn’t apply
  • Buy a local SIM at Tivat (€15–25 for 10–20GB) or use an eSIM

Croatia

Korçula Island

The best option if we specifically want an island holiday with wine culture. Korçula produces indigenous grapes you won’t find anywhere else — Grk (almost unique to Lumbarda), Pošip, and Plavac Mali. The food scene runs from Michelin-starred LD to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Crowds are real in August but manageable from a rural base.

Wine guide

The grapes: Grk (white, grows only in Lumbarda, sells out by October), Pošip (full-bodied white from Cara & Smokvica, Croatia’s most celebrated), Plavac Mali (robust red, related to Zinfandel), Rukatac (ancient white, 1,000+ years on the island).

Where to taste: On the island: Bire, Vitis, Sabulum, Popic (all small family operations, €12–20). “Slurp of Grk” guided tour from Korçula Explorer. On Pelješac (15-min ferry): Grgic Vina (Napa legend, walk-ins welcome), Matusko (Dingaç cellars), Edivo (underwater winery — wines aged 14m below sea), Korta Katarina (at the ferry terminal).

Montenegro

Ulcinj

The wildcard. Montenegro’s most southerly town, near the Albanian border, with a completely different character to the rest of the coast. Over 70% of the population is ethnically Albanian. Ottoman old town on the UNESCO Tentative List. And Velika Plaža: a 14km stretch of fine sand — extraordinary and rare anywhere on the Adriatic. Zero cruise ships, genuinely local food at half the price, and our budget is borderline overkill here.

Cultural note: Ulcinj is an Albanian town inside Montenegro. You’ll see Albanian signs, hear the call to prayer, and eat Albanian dishes not found elsewhere in Montenegro. The Albanian hospitality code (besa) means locals are unusually warm to visitors.

Ada Bojana & Bojana River

Destination restaurant
Miško
Oldest fish restaurant on Ada Bojana. Legendary fish soup, octopus carpaccio, black risotto. Wooden cabin with river views. Lobster tank. Worth the trip even from Budva.
Kod Ranka (Ranko’s Tavern)
Stilt house on the opposite bank. Famous for škampi na buzaru (shrimps braised in wine and garlic). Access by boat. Extraordinary setting.

Valdanos area

Mashtra Restaurant
Ancient olive grove terrace above Valdanos inlet. Fresh Adriatic fish, seasonal pasta. The finest setting in the region. Book ahead for August.

Dishes to seek out: bamije (veal & okra), riblja çorba (fish stew), škampi na buzaru, burek (filo pastry — get it from Albanian bakeries for breakfast), octopus carpaccio.

Beach guide

Velika Plaža (Long Beach)
12–13km of continuous fine grey sand. Longest on the Adriatic. Extremely shallow for 50–100m — ideal for everyone. Northern end is family-friendly; southern end is the kitesurfing zone. The scale means it never feels overcrowded. Mum and Dad can relax on the northern stretch while the rest of us kitesurf at the south end.
Ada Bojana
1,880m of soft sand on a river island at the Albanian border. Swim in the river (fresh) or the sea (salt). Wooden pile-dwelling houses and fish restaurants right on the water. Horseback riding at sunset on the dunes.
Valdanos
800m pebble in a sheltered cove. Surrounded by 100,000+ ancient olive trees, some 2,000 years old. Low crowds, great snorkelling. The only beach with genuine shade. Combine with Mashtra lunch above.

The heat

August in Ulcinj: 29–32°C average, peaks of 35–38°C. The fix: adopt the local siesta rhythm. Beach 8am–12pm, villa + AC from 12pm–5pm, evening activities from 5pm. Dinner at 8–10pm (normal local hour). AC in all bedrooms is non-negotiable for Mum and Dad.

Day trips

Stari Bar
30km, ~35 min. Ruins of a medieval walled city. Open-air museum of remarkable scale — fortress, cathedral ruins, Ottoman hammam. Plus the 2,000-year-old olive tree. Easy half-day, very low tourist footfall.
Skadar
45–60 min to Virpazar. Largest lake in the Balkans. Boat tours with pelicans, water lilies, island monasteries. Cooler on the water than on shore. Local Vranac red wine at Virpazar.
Shkodër
~30km, 45–60 min with border. Closest Albanian city. Rozafa Castle, Old Bazaar for coffee and street food. Check car rental allows Albania — some charge €20–30 supplement, others prohibit it. Most culturally distinctive day trip.

So what do we think?

Sveti Stefan / Przno is our recommendation. Beautiful beaches we can walk to, excellent local restaurants, authentic fishing-village character, no cruise ships, no glitz, and the budget stretches 20–25% further than Croatia. Korçula is the pick if we want a Croatian island with wine culture. Ulcinj is the adventure option. All three would be a brilliant week.
