Your mum suggested nine locations across Montenegro and Croatia. We researched every one against what the group actually wants: authentic coastal character, good beaches, great restaurants within 30 minutes, no cruise-ship crowds, and no oligarch glitz.

Three locations survived. Here they are, ranked.

The fishing village of Przno, ten minutes north of Budva but a world apart, ticks every box. Fishermen still work from the beach in the morning. Four distinct beaches are within walking distance. The restaurant scene — anchored by Drago (est. 1967) — is genuinely excellent. Zero cruise ships. And your £7–10k budget buys you the top tier of Montenegrin villa stock, because Montenegro is 23% cheaper than Croatia.

Glitz check: The Aman resort on Sveti Stefan island is ultra-luxury but discreetly so — invisible if you’re not a guest. The public beach flanking the causeway was open and free as of 2024. The surrounding area is authentic fishing village, not oligarch territory.

Getting There

  • easyJet direct from Gatwick/Luton to Tivat
  • Flight: ~2h 45min
  • Transfer: ~60 min by car
  • BA also flies Heathrow–Tivat 3x/week

Budget Reality

  • 5-bed villa with heated pool & sea views: confirmed ~£5,900/wk
  • £7–10k = top 10–15% of all MNE rental stock
  • Montenegro 23% cheaper than Croatia overall
  • Restaurant dinners roughly half Croatian prices

Beaches

  • Sveti Stefan Beach — pink sand crescent
  • Queen’s Beach — sheltered cove, cypress trees
  • Miloçer Beach — quieter, tree-lined
  • Przno Beach — village beach, restaurants above

Key Restaurants

  • Drago (Przno) — since 1967, local institution
  • Femelja Kentera — terraced hillside, island views
  • Çatovića Mlini (30 min) — rated Montenegro’s best
  • Hotel Conte (Perast) — benchmark bay seafood

If the group specifically wants an island holiday with wine culture, Korçula is the answer. The island produces indigenous grapes you won’t find anywhere else — Grk (almost unique to Lumbarda), Pošip, and Plavac Mali. The food scene runs from Michelin-starred LD Restaurant to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Crowds are real in August but manageable if you’re villa-based outside the old town, visiting in the evening after cruise boats leave.

Croatia costs more. Your £7–10k budget is realistic but at the ceiling for a 5-bed pool villa in August. Croatia has seen ~50% price inflation over three years and is now the second most expensive Mediterranean destination after France.

Getting There

  • Fly to Split or Dubrovnik (both very well served)
  • Split route: drive to Orebic, 15-min car ferry to Korçula
  • ~2–2.5h total from Split Airport
  • Dubrovnik route: ~2.5h via Pelješac

Budget Reality

  • £7–10k gets a quality 5–6 bed pool villa
  • Not enormous choice — book early
  • Dining: €80+ for two at upscale spots
  • Croatia 23% pricier than Montenegro

Beaches

  • Pupnatska Luka — white pebble, turquoise water, beach bar
  • Vela Pržina (Lumbarda) — sandy, family-friendly
  • Bilin Žal (Lumbarda) — sandy, shallow
  • Baçva — secluded cliff-backed cove

Key Restaurants

  • LD Restaurant — Michelin-starred
  • Konoba Adio Mare — same family since 1974
  • Konoba Mate (Pupnat) — fig trees, family produce
  • Wine tours: Grk & Pošip in Smokvica & Cara

The wildcard. Montenegro’s most southerly town, near the Albanian border, with a completely different character to the rest of the coast. Strong Ottoman/Albanian cultural influence, a cliff-top old town with genuine history, and — crucially — Velika Plaža: a 14km stretch of fine sand that is extraordinary and rare on the Adriatic. Zero cruise ships, genuinely local food at half the price of the Budva Riviera, and your £7–10k budget is borderline overkill here.

The caveats: 1.5 hours from Tivat airport. Infrastructure is rawer — not polished resort territory. Villa stock is thinner and less well-documented by UK agencies. Very hot in August (35°C+). But if the group wants maximum authenticity, this is it.

Getting There

  • Fly to Tivat (easyJet/BA direct)
  • Drive south: ~1.5 hours (72km)
  • Road is coastal, scenic but slow
  • Car essential here

Budget Reality

  • £7–10k is overkill — exceptional value
  • Fresh fish from €25/kg
  • Restaurant dinners very affordable
  • Could easily spend £5k and live like royalty

Beaches

  • Velika Plaža — 14km fine sand, extraordinary
  • Valdanos — sheltered pebble, emerald water
  • Ada Bojana — river island, wild and quiet
  • Mala Plaža — town beach (mixed reviews on cleanliness)

Key Restaurants

  • Dulcinea — cliff-top views, consistently praised
  • Il Galeone — chef celebrated on national TV
  • Teuta — traditional Albanian-Montenegrin dishes
  • Old Town waterfront has several authentic spots

Practical Notes

Book Early

August peak = quality 5-bed villas with pools book 4–6 months ahead. Don’t wait past February.

Car Rental

Essential at all three recommended locations. Book with the villa, not at the airport desk.

Booking Platforms

montenegrovillas.com (local specialist), oliverstravels.com, CV Villas, croatianvillaholidays.com

Flights

easyJet to Tivat (Montenegro), Split (Braç), Dubrovnik (Korçula) — all direct from London.

The Bottom Line

Sveti Stefan / Przno is the answer. It’s the rare spot that satisfies everyone in a mixed-age group: beautiful beaches the parents can walk to, excellent local restaurants, authentic fishing-village character, no cruise ships, no oligarch glitz, and the budget stretches 20–25% further than Croatia. Korçula is the backup if the group wants a Croatian island with wine culture. Skip Kotor, Budva, Tivat, and Krk entirely in August.
