Three places survived

Out of twelve options, only three genuinely fit what we’re after: authentic character, walkable beaches, great food, no cruise ships, no oligarch glitz, and villas in budget.

01
Montenegro

Sveti Stefan / Przno

The fishing village of Przno, ten minutes north of Budva but a world apart. Fishermen still work from the beach in the morning. Four beaches within walking distance, a genuinely excellent restaurant scene, zero cruise ships. And our budget buys the top tier here — Montenegro is 23% cheaper than Croatia.

easyJet to Tivat, 2h 45m direct Transfer: ~1h by car Villa: ~£5,900/wk confirmed

02
Croatia

Korçula Island

The pick if we want an island holiday with wine culture. Indigenous grapes you won’t find anywhere else — Grk, Pošip, Plavac Mali. Food runs from Michelin-starred LD to family konobas with tables under fig trees. Crowds are real in August but manageable from a rural villa.

Fly to Dubrovnik, ~20 weekly departures Transfer: ~4.5h (minibus + ferry) Villa: £5,800–10k/wk range

03
Montenegro

Ulcinj

The wildcard. Near the Albanian border, completely different character to the rest of the coast. Over 70% ethnically Albanian, Ottoman old town on the UNESCO Tentative List. And Velika Plaža: 14km of fine sand — extraordinary and rare on the Adriatic. Zero cruise ships, genuinely local food at half the price, and our budget is borderline overkill here.

easyJet to Tivat, 2h 45m direct Transfer: ~1.5h south by car Villa: £1,400–5k/wk (budget is overkill)

What about Mum’s other suggestions?

Here’s what we found for every place on the original list.

Montenegro
Perast
Day trip
Stunning Venetian village. Excellent restaurants. But no beaches and almost no villa stock — visit for dinner from a base elsewhere.
Kotor
Avoid
4–6 cruise ships daily in August. 15,000+ day-trippers into a town of 23,000. Restaurant owners close up because “the season kills the magic.”
Luštica Bay
Wrong fit
Not the cruise ship dock (that’s Kotor). But a purpose-built luxury resort, still partly under construction. One reviewer: “a soulless ghost town.”
Budva
Avoid
Montenegro’s party capital. 45% of all its tourism. Packed sun-loungers, loud music, Eastern European party crowd.
Tivat
Fails brief
Porto Montenegro marina = documented oligarch yacht parking. Russian superyachts reported here even during sanctions.
Kolašin
Day trip
Beautiful mountains, genuinely peaceful. But not coastal. Worth an excursion — Biogradska Gora is one of Europe’s last virgin forests.
Croatian Islands
Braç
Backup
Best logistics (50-min ferry from Split). Deepest villa stock. But Zlatni Rat beach is a zoo in August. Base near Milna, not Bol.
Lošinj
Awkward
Least crowded island. But no commercial airport, nearly four hours from Rijeka, thinner villa stock, food scene drifts Japanese/Greek.
Krk
Eliminate
Bridge to the mainland means car-crowded mass tourism. “Insanely touristy” in August. Wrong crowd entirely.

Practical notes

Book early

August peak — quality 5-bed villas book 4–6 months ahead. Don’t wait past February.

Car rental

Essential at all three picks. Book with the villa, not at the airport desk.

Where to book

montenegrovillas.com, oliverstravels.com, CV Villas, croatianvillaholidays.com

Flights

easyJet to Tivat (Montenegro) or Split/Dubrovnik (Croatia). All direct from London.

So what do we think?

Sveti Stefan / Przno is our recommendation. Beautiful beaches we can walk to, excellent local restaurants, authentic fishing-village character, no cruise ships, no glitz, and the budget stretches 20–25% further than Croatia. Korçula is the pick if we want a Croatian island with wine culture. Ulcinj is the adventure option. All three would be a brilliant week.
