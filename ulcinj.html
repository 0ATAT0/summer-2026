Ulcinj

An Albanian town inside Montenegro. Over 70% of Ulcinj’s population is ethnically Albanian. We’ll see Albanian signs, hear the call to prayer, and eat Albanian dishes not found elsewhere in Montenegro. The Albanian hospitality code (besa) means locals are unusually warm to visitors. The Old Town (Stari Grad) is on the UNESCO Tentative List.

Restaurant guide

Old Town (Stari Grad)

Must-visit
Antigona
Cliffside terrace with panoramic sea views. Famous for sunsets. Fresh daily seafood — octopus salad, sharing platters. Arrive early in summer; no online booking.
Dulcinea
Family-run, Old Town hotel setting. Refined Montenegrin specialties, harbour views.
Taphana
Wood-fired steaks and Adriatic cuisine. Old Town atmosphere.
Fisherman Hari
Cliffside above fishing grounds. Daily catch — sea bass, eel, bream. Genuinely local.

Town & waterfront

La Guitarra
Signature dish: bamije (veal and okra stew — genuinely Albanian, we won’t find it elsewhere in Montenegro). Mediterranean garden setting.
Bazar
Best value in town. Pasta €4, pizza €5, fish €25/kg. Genuinely local crowd. Not glamorous but authentic.
Higo
Premium steaks, Wagyu/Kobe. Set in Pinješ pine forest above a cove. Our celebratory dinner spot.

Ada Bojana & Bojana River

Destination restaurant
Miško
Oldest fish restaurant on Ada Bojana. Legendary fish soup, octopus carpaccio, black risotto. Wooden cabin with river views, lobster tank. Worth the trip even from Budva.
Kod Ranka (Ranko’s Tavern)
Stilt house on the opposite bank. Famous for škampi na buzaru (shrimps braised in wine and garlic). Access by boat. Extraordinary setting.

Valdanos area

Mashtra Restaurant
At The Olive House. Ancient olive grove terrace above Valdanos inlet. Fresh fish, seasonal pasta. Finest setting in the region. Book ahead for August.
Signature dishes to seek out: bamije (veal & okra), riblja çorba (fish stew), škampi na buzaru, burek (filo pastry — from Albanian bakeries for breakfast), octopus carpaccio.

Beach guide

Six beaches, from the longest sand stretch on the Adriatic to secluded marine reserves. Something for everyone — literally.

Velika Plaža (Long Beach)
12–13km of continuous fine grey sand. Longest on the Adriatic. Extremely shallow for 50–100m — ideal for everyone. Northern end is family-friendly; southern end is the kitesurfing zone. The sheer scale means it never feels overcrowded. Mum and Dad on the north, rest of us kitesurf at the south.
Mala Plaža (Town Beach)
376m crescent of fine sand directly below the Old Town walls. Walking distance from restaurants. Packed in August — arrive early.
Ada Bojana
1,880m of soft sand on a river island at the Albanian border. Swim in the river (fresh) or the sea (salt). Wooden pile-dwelling houses, fish restaurants right on the water. Horseback riding at sunset on the dunes.
Valdanos
800m pebble in a sheltered cove. Surrounded by 100,000+ ancient olive trees, some 2,000 years old. Low crowds, great snorkelling. Only beach with genuine shade. Combine with a Mashtra lunch above.
Crystal Beach
Pristine secluded cove. Marine Protected Area. Access by boat or footpath. No facilities. For the younger group.
Liman I & II
Small coves below the castle walls. Pebble/concrete, ladder access. Swimming under medieval walls.
August heat: 29–32°C average, peaks 35–38°C. Adopt the local siesta rhythm: beach 8am–12pm, villa + AC from 12pm–5pm, evening activities from 5pm, dinner 8–10pm. Valdanos has natural shade from the olive groves. AC in all bedrooms is non-negotiable for Mum and Dad.

Day trips

Stari Bar
30km, ~35 min. Medieval walled city ruins — an open-air museum of remarkable scale. Fortress, cathedral ruins, Ottoman hammam. Plus the 2,000-year-old olive tree. Easy half-day.
Skadar
45–60 min to Virpazar. Largest lake in the Balkans. Boat tours with pelicans, water lilies, island monasteries. Cooler on the water than on shore. Local Vranac wine at Virpazar.
Shkodër
~30km, 45–60 min with border. Closest Albanian city. Rozafa Castle, Old Bazaar, Mesi Bridge. Check car rental allows Albania — some charge €20–30 supplement, others prohibit it entirely. Most culturally distinctive day trip we can do.
Sveti Stefan
1.5–2h north. Montenegro’s most photographed view. Combine with Budva Old Town for an afternoon wander.

Practical

Getting there

  • Tivat Airport to Ulcinj: ~120km, 1.5–2h drive south
  • Consider private transfer (~€80–120)
  • Coastal road, paved but two-lane

Language

  • Albanian is the primary spoken language
  • English sufficient for tourism
  • Useful: Faleminderit (thank you)
  • Useful: Ju lutem (please)

Money

  • Euro (Montenegro’s currency)
  • Cards accepted at major restaurants
  • Cash for bakeries, market stalls, boats
  • ATMs in central Ulcinj

Car rental

  • Essential — collect at Tivat airport
  • If crossing to Albania, confirm the rental agreement permits it
  • Some charge €20–30 supplement for Albania

Sample week

Enough structure to hit every highlight, loose enough that Mum and Dad can swap a day trip for a pool day whenever they like.

Day 1
Arrive. Transfer from Tivat (~1h45). Pool. Sunset swim at Mala Plaža. Dinner at Bazar — fish by the kilo, no fuss.
Day 2
Old Town. 8–11am: Stari Grad ramparts, castle, Corsairs Museum. Albanian burek for breakfast. Swim below the walls. Siesta. Evening: Antigona for sunset.
Day 3
Long Beach + kites. Velika Plaža by 8am. Mum and Dad on the northern stretch, rest of us at the kitesurfing school (~€60–80 intro lesson). Siesta. Evening: La Guitarra — order the bamije.
Day 4
Valdanos + Mashtra. Morning snorkelling at Valdanos. Walk up to Mashtra for a long olive-grove lunch — the meal of the week. Pool afternoon.
Day 5
Stari Bar + Skadar. Ruins and the 2,000-year-old olive tree (morning). Drive to Virpazar for a lake boat trip. Vranac wine. Evening: Miško on Ada Bojana.
Day 6
Shkodër, Albania. Leave 8am. Rozafa Castle, Old Bazaar, Mesi Bridge. Back by 2pm. Pool. Evening: Dulcinea in the Old Town.
Day 7
Ada Bojana. Walk the island. Swim where the river meets the sea. Horseback riding at sunset (~€25–40/hr). Farewell: Kod Ranka — stilt house on the river, škampi na buzaru.
Day 8
Depart. Allow 2+ hours to Tivat Airport.
